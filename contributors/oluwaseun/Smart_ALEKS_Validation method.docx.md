**Validation Approach** 

The gold standard for validation is dual human review. In alignment with this standard, we employ a two-stage human validation process to ensure that the output generated by the SMART-ALEKS pipeline are accurate, complete, and appropriate for release to district policymakers and stakeholders. Both stages are human-led and operate at distinct points in the pipeline, each serving a different purpose. The first stage validates the structured data before any narrative is generated; the second validates the generated Research Brief before it is released to the district.

* **Stage 1 Validation**: Human review of the extracted XLSX data  
* **Stage 2 Validation**: Human review of the generated Research Brief

This two-stage design reflects a deliberate separation of concerns. Errors caught at Stage 1 are data errors — wrong values, missing fields, or misextracted figures — and are corrected before they propagate into any generated narrative. Errors caught at Stage 2 are output errors — narrative claims, framing, or visualizations that do not accurately represent the validated data. Addressing each type of error at the right point in the pipeline keeps corrections targeted, minimises rework, and ensures that every release-ready Research Brief rests on a foundation of human-verified structured data.

**Pipeline Context**

To understand where each validation stage sits, it is necessary to describe the pipeline as a whole. The pipeline begins with individual RCE report PDFs drawn from LearnPlatform. These PDFs are passed to Agent 1 — the LLM generation step — which reads each report and extracts its structured data into an XLSX file. This XLSX becomes the single source of truth for all downstream generation. It is at this point that Stage 1 validation occurs.

Once the XLSX has been validated and confirmed correct, the evaluator determines whether historical data exists for the same district-product combination from prior evaluation cycles. If previous reports are available, they are incorporated to provide longitudinal context. The validated XLSX — with or without historical enrichment — then passes to Agent 2, which generates the Research Brief narrative and the associated data visualizations. The Research Brief presents findings at multiple levels of granularity: a birds-eye district view as well as breakdowns by school, grade, and population. It is at this point that Stage 2 validation occurs.

Only after both stages have been cleared does the Research Brief proceed to release.

**Stage 1 Validation – Human review of extracted data**

Stage 1 validation is the first checkpoint in the pipeline and the most consequential. Because every downstream output — the Research Brief, the data visualizations, and the district-level summaries — is generated directly from the extracted XLSX, an error in that file propagates into everything the pipeline produces. Catching and correcting data errors at this stage, before generation begins, is therefore more efficient than identifying them after the fact in a generated narrative.

**What is being reviewed**

The reviewer opens the XLSX file produced by Agent 1 and works through the extracted fields directly in Excel. The review is a field-level check: the reviewer compares the extracted values against the source RCE PDF reports to confirm that each field has been correctly captured. The fields subject to review include all variables in the extraction schema — effect sizes, sample sizes, ESSA tier designations, usage metrics and thresholds, subgroup findings, etc.

The reviewer is not assessing writing quality or narrative framing at this stage. The sole focus is whether the structured data values are correct. This is a deliberate constraint: it keeps the Stage 1 review task fast, precise, and separable from the judgment calls that belong to Stage 2\.

**How corrections are made**

When the reviewer identifies an incorrect value, they correct it directly in the Excel file. There is no separate correction interface and no resubmission loop. The reviewer edits the cell, records a brief note in a designated word document explaining what was wrong and what the correct value is, and continues to the next field. All corrections are made within a single review session. When the reviewer is satisfied that the XLSX is accurate, they provide the correction document to Agent 1 for reinforced learning, validate that it has understood and applied the corrections, and once validated will pass the XLSX to the next pipeline stage.

This direct-in-Excel correction approach is intentional. It keeps the correction mechanism as simple and transparent as possible, produces a self-documenting record of every change made within the file itself, helps reinforce learning for Agent 1, and avoids introducing additional tooling between the reviewer and the data.

**Historical data enrichment**

Once the XLSX has been validated, the researcher specifies whether previous RCE reports exist for the same district-product combination from prior evaluation cycles. If historical data is available, it is incorporated into the validated XLSX to provide longitudinal context before the Research Brief is generated. This allows Agent 2 to situate current findings within a broader trajectory — for example, noting whether an effect size has grown, declined, or remained stable relative to a previous cycle. If no historical data exists for a given combination, the pipeline proceeds with the current cycle's data alone.

The historical enrichment step occurs after Stage 1 validation is complete, not before. This sequencing ensures that the reviewer's data check is conducted on the current extraction only, without the complexity of distinguishing current from historical values during review.

**Stage 2 Validation – Human review of the Research Brief**

Stage 2 validation occurs after Agent 2 has generated the Research Brief and its associated data visualizations. At this point the structured data has already been human-validated in Stage 1\. The focus of Stage 2 therefore shifts from data accuracy to output quality: the reviewer assesses whether the generated narrative faithfully represents the validated data, whether the visualizations are correctly labelled and appropriately interpreted, and whether the brief as a whole is suitable for release to district stakeholders.

**What is being reviewed**

The reviewer reads the full Research Brief, attending to the following dimensions:

* Narrative accuracy: every factual claim in the brief — effect sizes, sample sizes, ESSA tier designations, subgroup findings — is consistent with the validated XLSX.  
* Completeness: all significant findings are represented, including subgroups that did not reach significance or had insufficient data, which must be acknowledged rather than silently omitted.  
* Appropriate framing: findings are described accurately with respect to their directionality and magnitude, and no claim overstates the strength of evidence beyond what the ESSA tier designation supports.  
* Visualization accuracy: charts, tables, and breakdowns by school, grade, and district level correctly reflect the underlying data and are legible for a non-technical policymaker audience.  
* Longitudinal framing: where historical data has been incorporated, the brief accurately characterises trends across cycles without overstating continuity or change.

**How corrections are made**

When the reviewer identifies an issue in the Research Brief, the appropriate correction path depends on the nature and scale of the error.

* **Minimal revision:** if the issue is a minor wording error, a labelling problem in a visualization, or a small narrative inaccuracy that does not reflect a data error in the XLSX, the reviewer corrects it directly in the Research Brief document. This path is used when the error is isolated, the correct formulation is unambiguous, and no data-level change is required.  
* **Return to XLSX stage:** if the issue reflects an underlying error in the validated XLSX — for example, a finding that was missed during Stage 1 review, or a value that was correctly extracted but incorrectly used in generation — the reviewer returns the pipeline to Stage 1\. The XLSX is corrected, re-validated, and Agent 2 regenerates the Research Brief from the corrected data. The regenerated brief then re-enters Stage 2 validation.

This two-path correction structure is deliberate. It avoids the overhead of a full pipeline re-run for issues that can be resolved at the output level, while ensuring that errors rooted in the data are always addressed at source rather than patched in the narrative.

**Stage 2 release criteria**

| Scenario | Condition | Action |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Valid — released** | Narrative accurately represents the validated XLSX; all findings are complete; visualizations are correct; framing is appropriate for a policymaker audience | Research Brief is released to the district as the final output. |
| **Valid after minimal revision** | Reviewer identified and corrected minor wording, framing, or visualization issues directly in the brief without data-level changes | Revised Research Brief is released to the district. Revisions are noted in the audit log. |
| **Returned to Stage 1** | Reviewer identified an issue rooted in the XLSX data rather than the generated narrative | Pipeline returns to Stage 1\. XLSX is corrected and re-validated. Agent 2 regenerates the brief. Re-enters Stage 2 validation. |
| **Invalid — generation failure** | Narrative errors are pervasive or systemic, indicating Agent 2's generation prompt failed rather than producing isolated issues | Research Brief is not released. Generation prompt is reviewed and revised. Agent 2 re-runs on the validated XLSX after prompt revision. |

**Audit trail and records**

Both validation stages generate a record that is retained as part of the pipeline's audit log. At Stage 1, the corrected XLSX itself serves as the primary record: all reviewer corrections are documented in a word document which is used later to retrain Agent 1 (LLM), providing a field-level account of what was changed and why. At Stage 2, the reviewer logs any corrections made directly to the Research Brief, noting the nature of the issue and the correction path taken — whether the brief was revised directly or returned to Stage 1\. These records serve two purposes. **First,** they provide a complete audit trail for each batch, allowing any released Research Brief to be traced back through both validation stages to the source PDF data. **Second**, they constitute a growing dataset of human-identified errors at both the extraction and generation levels. Over time, patterns in this dataset inform targeted revisions to the Agent 1 extraction prompt and the Agent 2 generation prompt, progressively reducing the rate of errors that require human correction in future cycles.
